Things you did not know about giraffes

It's time to learn some few interesting things about giraffes!

Here we are going to learn some few facts about giraffes. The giraffe is a tall African mammal belonging to the genus Giraffa. specifically, It is an even-toed ungulate. It is the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant on Earth.
Here I am going to try and add an unordered list of some facts about giraffes:

  • Giraffes are the tallest mammals on Earth. Their legs alone are taller than many humans.
  • They can run as fast as 35 miles an hour over short distances, or cruise at 10 mph over longer distances.
  • A giraffe's neck is too short to reach the ground. As a result, it has to awkwardly spread its front legs or kneel to reach the ground for a drink of water.
  • Giraffes only need to drink once every few days. Most of their water comes from all the plants they eat.
  • Giraffes spend most of their lives standing up; they even sleep and give birth standing up.
